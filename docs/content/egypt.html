Egyptian Soul Architecture

Ancient Egyptians pictured a person as a network of bodily and spiritual parts. Preservation of the body and ethical living allowed these facets to reunite after death.

  • Ka: the life-force that required offerings to endure.
  • Ba: a mobile personality, often shown as a human-headed bird visiting the tomb each night.
  • Akh: an enlightened spirit formed when Ka and Ba were harmonized.

Mummification, offering cults, and the Opening of the Mouth ceremony animated these elements, leading to the weighing of the heart against the feather of Ma'at.
