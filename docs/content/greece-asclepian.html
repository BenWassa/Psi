Asclepian Dream Healing

Sanctuaries of Asclepius functioned as holistic hospitals. After purification, patients slept in the abaton where sacred snakes roamed and silence set the stage for divine dreams.

  • Visions of the god offered cures or symbolic prescriptions interpreted by temple priests.
  • Inscriptions known as iamata celebrated recoveries from blindness, paralysis and other ailments.
  • The ritual environment and expectation of epiphany worked together as an early form of psychotherapy.
