Mesopotamian Dream Traditions

In ancient Mesopotamia, dreams were treated as direct channels from the divine. The soul was believed to wander beyond the body at night, receiving messages that guided both kings and commoners.

Narrative Highlights

  • Epic of Gilgamesh: Foreshadowing Enkidu's arrival and offering guidance on dangerous quests.
  • Gudea of Lagash: Visionary commands to build temples legitimized political authority.
  • Dumuzi's Prophecy: Detailed symbols interpreted by his sister predicted his downfall.

These accounts reveal a culture where dream interpreters translated nocturnal visions into statecraft, ritual, and personal transformation.
