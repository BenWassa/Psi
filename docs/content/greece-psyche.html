Orphic and Platonic Soul

Orphic traditions taught that humans inherit a divine spark within a Titanic body, making life a tension between heavenly origin and earthly desire.

  • Ascetic rituals and remembrance of one's divine ancestry aimed to liberate the soul from cycles of rebirth.
  • Plato's tripartite psyche cast reason as a charioteer directing spirited and appetitive horses toward the realm of Forms.
  • Both perspectives urged purification and philosophical discipline to restore the soul's kinship with the divine.
